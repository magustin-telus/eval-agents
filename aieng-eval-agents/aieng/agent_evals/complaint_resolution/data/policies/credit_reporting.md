# id: POL-CREDIT-REPORTING
# title: Credit Reporting Complaint Resolution Policy
# category: credit_reporting

## Scope
Complaints about credit report accuracy: incorrect account information, accounts that do
not belong to the customer, outdated or duplicate entries, disputed late-payment marks,
incorrect balances, and the handling of identity-theft-related reporting.

## Investigation steps
1. Identify the specific disputed item(s) on the credit report (account, date, field).
2. Compare the reported information against the institution's internal records.
3. Determine whether the entry originated from this institution or a third party.
4. For identity theft, confirm whether a fraud report or police report exists.
5. Initiate a reinvestigation with the relevant credit bureau where required.

## Remediation
- Verified inaccuracies are corrected or deleted and a correction request is sent to the
  bureaus within 30 calendar days.
- Items confirmed as not belonging to the customer are removed and blocked from re-reporting.
- The customer is provided a written summary of the investigation result.
- Where a correction is made, an updated copy of the affected report section is offered.

## Escalation
- Disputes unresolved after 30 calendar days are escalated to the Credit Bureau Liaison team.
- Suspected identity theft is referred to the Fraud Investigations team.
- Repeated re-reporting of a corrected item is escalated to a compliance review.

## What we do not do
- We do not remove accurate, verifiable information solely because it is unfavorable.
- We do not alter records held by other institutions or bureaus on their behalf.
- We do not provide credit-repair services or guarantee score changes.
